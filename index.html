Introduction

Hi, I'm Yiping Li, a Machine Learning Researcher specializing in federated learning and model optimization, with a solid foundation in machine learning and applied mathematics.

I earned a Professional Master of Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and a dual major in Psychology and the Applied and Computational Mathematical Sciences (ACMS) from the University of Washington, building a strong foundation for interdisciplinary research.

My research focuses on model optimization, with a current emphasis on federated learning. I aim to address key challenges such as computational efficiency, privacy preservation, and resource-constrained deployments, striving to develop scalable and practical solutions that bridge theoretical advancements with real-world applications.

Since 2021, I have had the privilege of being a member of the Joint Laboratory of BUPT and Chuangcache, under the supervision of Professor Yuchao Zhang. My research in the lab focuses on federated learning, tackling challenges such as communication resource constraints and the Non-I.I.D. problem. Additionally, I have worked on projects combining knowledge distillation and curriculum learning to enhance teaching efficacy and model performance.

Currently, I am working on knowledge distillation on federated learning, aiming to reduce communication cost through gradient prediction. Feel free to reach out if you'd like to connect or discuss potential collaborations!
